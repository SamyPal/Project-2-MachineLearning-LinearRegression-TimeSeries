<h2>2019 U.S Small Car Sales Analysis</h2>
<p><strong>Report Updated: With all 2019 Sales Data on January 3rd, 2020</strong></p>
<p><strong>Top Level US Reports: By Manufacturer / <a href="https://www.goodcarbadcar.net/u-s-auto-sales-figures-by-brand/">By Brand</a> / <a href="https://www.goodcarbadcar.net/us-vehicle-sales-figures-by-model/">By Model</a> / <a href="https://www.goodcarbadcar.net/2019-us-best-selling-vehicles/">Best Sellers</a></strong></p>
<p>Small car sales in the U.S. struggled in the month of December and in the entire year of 2019. This should come as no surprise. The segment has struggled for the last several years and will likely continue to do so as time moves on.</p>
<p>The segment saw about 30,000 fewer sales in the month of December than it did in the same month in 2018. When you look at the annual sales numbers, you&#8217;ll see that it was down a whopping 186,000 sales.</p>
<p>The car the led the pack for the year was the Honda Civic. Despite its leading numbers, the model saw sales decline both in the month of December and in 2019 as a whole. That car was followed by the Toyota Corolla, which saw sales drop for the month but saw sales rise less than a percent for the year. In third place, was the Nissan Sentra, which saw dramatic sales declines in both the month of December and for 2019.</p><p><div class="block-da-1 block-da block-da-post_middle_content clearfix"><!-- Ezoic - GCBC_Zeen_Ad_Top_Mid_End_Post - sidebar -->
<div id="ezoic-pub-ad-placeholder-135"></div>
<!-- End Ezoic - GCBC_Zeen_Ad_Top_Mid_End_Post - sidebar --></div></p>
<p>There&#8217;s no reason to assume that small cars will be able to turn things around in 2020. We fully expect sales to continue to slip.</p>
<p><em><strong>Note: FCA US LLC will end its practice of reporting U.S. and Canada sales on a monthly basis following June results. FCA US and FCA Canada will report monthly sales for May and June. The first quarterly report will occur on October 1, 2019, covering the months of July, August, and September.</strong></em></p>
